Conclusion

Figure 2 and figure 3 compare the average violent crime rate and the average property crime rate between states without marijuana legalization and states with legalized marijuana.

It shows crime rates have reduced in the last 20 years. However, property crime rate among states with legalized marijuana reduces faster than states without marijuana legalization.

In the end, in the face of this research conclusion: the legalization of marijuana can alleviate the urgent economic and social problems facing some regions of the United States to a certain extent. But at the same time, the negative impact and new challenges that legalization brings to American society should not be underestimated. The legalization of marijuana runs counter to the US government's anti-drug national policy that has been implemented for nearly a century. Its spread will gradually engulf the anti-drug moral consensus and legal achievements that American society has achieved over the past century. In the face of this reality, the liberals who promoted the legalization of marijuana turned their attention to cannabis that many Americans regarded as "soft" drugs, and tried to use marijuana as a breakthrough to achieve the full legalization of drugs. The legalization of marijuana will further exacerbate the division of American society. The position of the American people in favor of and against the legalization of marijuana and different states on this issue is seriously divided. In addition, the continued advancement of the legalization of marijuana in the United States will inevitably cause serious damage to the global drug anti-drug action.
