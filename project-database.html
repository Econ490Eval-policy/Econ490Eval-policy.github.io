Project Database

The project is to identify the impact of marijuana legalization on criminal rates in the United States. Previous studies only compare two states after legalization using regression discontinuity methods. Different from previous studies, I would like to estimate the impact of marijuana legalization on criminal rates by using time-varying difference in difference method. That is, comparing states without legalized marijuana and states with legalized marijuana, and then identify its potential impact.

So, two different measure of criminal rates are used in the paper, violent crime and property crime, which can be downloaded form the FBI report. Given that the cost of marijuana acquisitions is lower and illegal activities related marijuana transaction reduce, legalized marijuana can lead to lower criminal rates. However, the impact of marijuana legalization on violent crime and property crime could be different. I can use both rates to compare the difference. As a result, the questions will be answer int the project are, does marijuana legalization lead to lower criminate rates, in terms of violent and property crimes? If yes, what is the difference of two criminal rates?

Data of this project are from different sources, FBI report, US statistics bureau and marijuana legalization policy website：

Independent variables include dummy variable to measure marijuana policy in the United States, which can be collected from US marijuana policy website. This website provides detail information about the timing of marijuana legalization process of each state in the United States （Contains data for 50 states from 1996 to 2019.）

Download: States ID and medical legalization.pdf

Dependent variable is state-level criminal rate from 2000 to 2018, which can be downloaded from the FBI website.

Other control variables are GDP growth, unemployment rate, population and law-enforcement officers. Law-enforcement officers’ data can be downloaded from the FBI website. Other data can be derived from US statistics bureau.
